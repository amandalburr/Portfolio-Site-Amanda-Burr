Shop Small

The Shop Small app (virtually) opens the doors to small businesses so users can learn more about the business, the owners and what makes them unique. As the UI designer for this project, I implemented techniques such as user testing and user personas to dictate the functionality of the app and design tools like moodboards and style guides to guide the visual direction.

My process

User Research

The user research part of the project began with interviewing those who have an interest in small businesses and their impact on communities. After the interviews were conducted, user personas were developed to reflect the needs of a typical user of Shop Small.

Rapid Prototyping

After I talked to potential users, created personas, answered essential questions and determined the jobs to be done, it was time to start drawing out my ideas on paper. Here I utilized the Crazy 8 method to rapidly create different screen versions. Once I completed that task, I selected my favorite, most successful designs and began to put together wireframes.

Wireframing

Wireframes help to demonstrate and test a user flow to ensure users can achieve their goals on the app. Having participants test at this state can help discover any blind spots. It’s better to discover them earlier, rather than later in the process. When I felt comfortable about the flow of my low-fidelity wireframes, I solidified my designs by creating mid-fidelity wireframes.

Moodboards

Once the UX portion of the project was completed, I began to conceptualize the look and feel of the app. I started by creating two mood boards to visually communicate the artistic direction of the project.

The first moodboard utilizes playful colors and illustrations and aims to showcase a ‘small town’ feel. Ultimately, this direction felt too playful and juvenile and was not selected for the final design.

The second moodboard provides a more serious tone and focuses more on the small business and the people behind them. The color palette is subdued and calming. This moodboard was selected for the final design.

Style Guide

Another tool used to conceptualize the look of the app is the style guide. This guide ensures consistency is applied across the design and contains everything from the color palette to the button styles contained within the app.

Responsive Design

After the look of the app is fully conceptualized, it’s time to start applying it to more finalized screens and mockups.

Shop Small was designed with a mobile-first mindset and adapted to four breakpoints. Below three screen sizes are displayed: mobile, tablet and laptop. Users will have a seamless experience whether they are viewing the app on a mobile device, tablet, laptop or desktop computer.

Features

I wanted the Shop Small app to be simple in terms of functionality but still contain features that will help users to learn about each business and to find one that fits their needs.

The apps main screens provide a warm, welcoming feeling.

Users can get personalized directions to visit the small business of their choice.

Shop Small features user-submitted reviews, ratings and keeps track of their favorite small businesses.
